## Bio

Feems is a funding design researcher and strategist focused on how decentralized ecosystems structure and deploy non-dilutive capital. Her work builds shared mental models for funding systems — translating complex capital architectures into design references that make decision-making clearer, alignment more achievable, and outcomes more comparable across programs and rounds.

She has worked across the full grants stack as a funder, operator, reviewer, researcher, and grantee, with ecosystems including Gitcoin, Arbitrum, POKT, and Polygon. She served on the Gitcoin Grants Council (GG20–22) and founded the DAO Governance Collective, one of the earliest education initiatives dedicated to DAO governance.

Her background bridges technical and non-technical domains — spanning UI and information architecture, product development, strategic communication, marketing, and public policy.

## Frameworks & Research

**CAMF — Capital Architecture Mapping Framework**
A system design model and taxonomy for non-dilutive funding that defines the roles, mechanisms, lifecycle phases, and decision anchors required to design and evolve decentralized funding systems as a coherent whole rather than a collection of standalone programs.

**Funding Design**
An emerging design discipline applying systems thinking and human-centered design to non-dilutive funding in decentralized, online coordination environments.

**Grant Maturity Index**
A framework for assessing the maturity and effectiveness of Web3 grant programs.

**Ecosystem Support Framework (ESF)**
A systems-level model for how ecosystems support builders and contributors over time through coordinated governance, capital, and non-financial support.

**Onchain Capital Allocation: Explorer Edition**
Served as developmental editor and co-writer with Kevin Owocki, contributing to foundational research on decentralized capital allocation.

## FlowOS

FlowOS is the direct product of this research — applying the CAMF lifecycle architecture to grant program coordination. It implements the Design → Fund → Assess loop as a working platform: structured program intent, AI-assisted intake, consistent review, and a registry that makes funded outcomes comparable across rounds.
